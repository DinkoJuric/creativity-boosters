### The SIT 5-Step Subtraction Process

```
STEP 1: LIST all components of your product/service
        â†“
STEP 2: SELECT one "essential" component to subtract
        â†“
STEP 3: IMAGINE the product without it (the "virtual product")
        â†“
STEP 4: IDENTIFY the "dangling function" â€” what role did it play?
        â†“
STEP 5: SOLVE â€” Find alternatives OR discover it wasn't needed
```

### Why This Works: Breaking Cognitive Fixedness

**Cognitive fixedness** = Our brain's tendency to see things only in their current form

| What We Assume | The Reality |
|----------------|-------------|
| Current components are "essential" | Many exist due to history, not need |
| Addition = progress | Addition often = complexity tax |
| Users want more features | Users want fewer problems |

### The "Dangling Function" Problem

When you remove something, its function needs to go somewhere:
- **Option A:** Another component takes over
- **Option B:** The user takes over (Muji/IKEA model)
- **Option C:** You discover it wasn't needed (PillPack removed confusion)

### Exercise 1: Essential Component List
1. List every component of your product/service
2. Star the ones you consider "untouchable"

### Exercise 2: The 80/20 Feature Audit

### Exercise 3: Subtraction Sprint (Team Activity)

- Each person picks one "sacred" component
